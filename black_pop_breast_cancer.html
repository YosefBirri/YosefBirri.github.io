Black Population & Breast Cancer Intervention

Project 6 examining Black population distribution across U.S. counties and disparities in breast cancer mortality.

Portfolio Home

Project Overview

This project investigates the spatial distribution of Black populations across U.S. counties and identifies priority areas for targeted breast cancer interventions. We also compare breast cancer mortality among Black women to other racial groups.

Methodology

  • Data: U.S. Census population data, CDC breast cancer mortality data
  • Software: QGIS, R
  • Analysis: Spatial joins, choropleth mapping, priority scoring
  • Visualization: Maps highlighting counties with high Black population and elevated mortality rates

Key Insights

  • Counties with higher Black population density often coincide with higher breast cancer mortality rates.
  • Identified priority intervention areas to improve health outcomes.
  • Spatial analysis highlights inequities in health resource allocation.
