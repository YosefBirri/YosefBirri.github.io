Projected Winter Temperature (2071–2090)

Lab 5 project analyzing projected winter temperatures across the U.S. using climate models.

Portfolio Home

Project Overview

This project explores projected winter temperature changes in the United States between 2071 and 2090 using climate model simulations. The goal is to identify regional patterns and assess potential impacts on ecosystems and human activity.

Methodology

  • Data: Downscaled climate model projections for 2071–2090
  • Software: QGIS, R
  • Analysis: Spatial interpolation, raster visualization, anomaly calculation
  • Visualization: Choropleth maps of temperature changes across U.S. regions

Key Insights

  • Northern regions show more significant winter warming compared to southern regions.
  • Mountainous areas experience smaller temperature increases due to elevation effects.
  • Urban heat islands amplify local warming in metropolitan areas.
